Cooperative AI — emergence in multi-agent reinforcement learning

How do communication constraints and reward design shape cooperation between learning agents? My dissertation studies emergent coordination in MARL systems — and finds that the obvious levers often pull the wrong way.

setup

PPO agents implemented in PyTorch inside PettingZoo cooperative environments. Two axes are varied systematically: the communication bandwidth available between agents, and the reward structure — from fully selfish to fully shared. Coordination is measured, not eyeballed.

the counterintuitive result

Selfish incentives produced the most cooperation. Shared rewards sound cooperative, but they blur credit assignment: no agent can tell which of its actions earned the reward, so the gradient signal washes out. Selfish rewards keep the signal sharp — and cooperation emerges anyway, because it is instrumentally useful rather than imposed.

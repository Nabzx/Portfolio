TandemRLVR — training reasoning agents to remain legible to weaker overseers

Stronger models shouldn't just be right — their reasoning should be checkable by something weaker than they are. TandemRLVR asks whether reasoning agents can be trained and evaluated not only for final-answer correctness, but for producing intermediate reasoning a weaker overseer can inspect, continue, and verify.

the core loop

task → senior reasoning → junior completion → verifier → metrics

A senior agent reasons partway through a task, then hands off. A junior agent — deliberately weaker — must pick up the trace and finish. A verifier scores the outcome. If the senior's reasoning is legible, the junior succeeds more often: legibility stops being an aesthetic and becomes a measurable, trainable quantity.

staging

  • Stages 1–2 — heuristic placeholder agents establish the harness, with fully deterministic baselines.
  • Stage 3 — real local LLM agents drop in; the heuristic agents stay on as controls.

why it matters

This is a scalable-oversight question. As models outgrow their overseers, final-answer correctness stops being auditable on its own. Reasoning that survives a weaker reader is reasoning you can actually trust — and RLVR-style training signals can select for it.

engineering discipline

Built as clean RL research infrastructure: modular agents, reproducible runs, disciplined evaluation, and experiment outputs you can diff. Developed toward Anthropic's Reinforcement Learning Fellows programme.

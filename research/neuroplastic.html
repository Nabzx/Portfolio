Neuroplastic collective intelligence

Can communication mechanisms borrowed from computational neuroscience improve how collectives of agents coordinate — and push them toward functional specialisation nobody designed in?

approach

A multi-agent framework with adaptive graph-based communication: who talks to whom is not fixed, but learned. Connection strengths update with Hebbian-inspired plasticity — channels that carry useful signal strengthen, idle ones decay — so the interaction topology reshapes itself during cooperative learning.

measurement

Emergence claims are cheap, so the evaluation isn't: coordination and specialisation are quantified with graph-theoretic and information-theoretic measures across cooperative benchmarks, against fixed-topology baselines.

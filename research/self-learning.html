On self-learning systems

An investigative preprint on systems that improve themselves — what "self-learning" actually means mechanically, and where the free lunches aren't.

the question

Most systems described as self-learning are just fitting parameters. The interesting cases are systems that alter their own learning process. The preprint works through what separates the two, leaning on algorithmic information theory for the vocabulary — written alongside completing Imperial's AIAI1 course on the subject.

where it led

The most productive idea to survive the study: allocate computation by approximating minimum description length. That lens became BidNet — a compute-aware architecture where experts bid for their own compute through a differentiable auction. Theory that motivated an architecture, not the other way round.
